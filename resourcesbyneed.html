  • Do2Learn

    Provides thousands of free pages with social skills and behavioral regulation activities and guidance, learning songs and games, communication cards, academic material, and transition guides for employment and life skills. You can check it out here.

  • Unique Learning System

    Provides a one‑of‑a‑kind program designed specifically to give students with complex learning needs meaningful access to the general education curriculum. You can check it out here.

Dyslexia (Difficulty with reading, writing, spelling, speaking)

  • Starfall

    Great website for younger kids who are learning to read with dyslexia, because it’s free, colourful, user-friendly, and it helps with phonics and phonemic awareness. You can check it out here.

  • Dyslexia Explained E-Book

    Free 87-page ebook written by the founder of "Nessy Reading and Spelling", Mike Jones. You can check it out here.

    Offers free K-12 math skills practice and games with real-time diagnostics, reccomendations, and rewards.You can check it out here.

  • Hand2Mind

  • Math Antics

    Provides students with videos explaining basic math concepts and printable worksheets for free. You can check it out here.

Dysgraphia (Difficulty with handwriting, spelling, organizing ideas)

    Award-winning curriculum that offers a NEW integrated print and digital approach to teach pencil grip, letter formation, and the literacy skills that are needed for print and cursive handwriting. You can check it out here.

  • Touch-Type Read and Spell

    Multi-sensory course that teaches typing, reading and spelling. You can check it out here.

Dyspraxia (Difficulty with hand-eye coordination, balance, fine motor skills)

  • Link that Gugl Match

    A game that requires players to keep their eyes and finger coordinated in order to create the longest chain of the same color You can check it out on the app store here.

  • 20twentee

    An app specifically designed to help players work on their hand-eye coordination skills. Kids have one minute to hit the numbers from 1 to 20 as often as possible. You can check it out here.

  • Additional Physical Game Ideas

    This blog suggests a variety of games for kids of all ages to further develop their coordination skills. You can check it out here.

Auditory processing disorder (Difficulty interpreting what the ear hears)

  • Fast ForWord

    An evidence-based, adaptive reading and language program that delivers 1-2 years gain in 40-60 hours of use for any struggling learner. You can check it out here.

  • AudioNote

    Students can record lectures and type or draw notes at the same time. Helpful for kids who struggle with writing and organizing their notes while listening. You can check it out here.

  • Bookshare

    A leading source for free digital text-to-speech books. Your child can read textbooks, children’s classics, current bestsellers, and magazines. You can check it out here.

Visual processing issues (Difficulty interpreting what the eye sees)

  • Sensory World

    A website presenting a virtual, interactive house filled with activities designed to stimulate learning. You can check it out here.

    Puzzles are known for promoting visual cognitive skills. Choose a level that challenges, but doesn’t frustrate your child. here.
